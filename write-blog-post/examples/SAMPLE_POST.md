# How to Find Long-Tail Keywords in 10 Minutes

Long-tail keywords make up the majority of search queries and convert at a
higher rate than head terms. If you've ever stared at a blank keyword tool
wondering where to start, this 10-minute workflow will give you a ranked
list of terms you can actually write for.

## What counts as a long-tail keyword

A long-tail keyword is a specific, usually 4+ word search phrase with low
monthly volume but high intent. "Best running shoes" is a head term.
"Best running shoes for flat feet on pavement" is a long-tail term.

## The 10-minute workflow

### 1. Pull seed topics from your pillar page

Open the pillar page you want to support and list the subtopics it already
covers. Each subtopic is a seed.

### 2. Expand with a free keyword tool

Paste each seed into a tool like Google Keyword Planner or AnswerThePublic.
Export the questions and related phrases.

### 3. Score by intent and difficulty

Keep terms with:
- Monthly volume between 100 and 1,000
- Low-to-medium keyword difficulty
- Clear informational or commercial intent

### 4. Cluster and prioritize

Group related terms into one article each. Write the cluster with the
highest total volume first.

## Common mistakes

- **Chasing volume over intent.** A term with 50 monthly searches that
  converts is worth more than 5,000 searches that bounce.
- **Skipping the SERP check.** If the top results are all e-commerce pages
  and you're writing a guide, you won't rank — pick a different term.

## Conclusion

You now have a repeatable way to surface long-tail keywords in ten minutes.
Run it once a month, feed the winners into your content calendar, and watch
the targeted traffic compound.
