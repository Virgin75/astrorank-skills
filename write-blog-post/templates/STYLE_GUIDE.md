# Style guide

Rules for the prose itself. Apply these during drafting and final review.

## Voice

- Write like a knowledgeable colleague talking to a peer.
- Second person ("you") over third person ("users").
- Active voice over passive. "The tool ranks pages" not "Pages are ranked."
- Confident, not hyped. Avoid "revolutionary", "game-changing", "ultimate".

## Sentences and paragraphs

- Average sentence length ~15 words. Mix short (5–8) and long (up to 25).
- One idea per paragraph. Max 4 sentences.
- No filler openers: "In today's fast-paced world", "It goes without saying",
  "At the end of the day", "Believe it or not".

## Word choice

- Plain language. No jargon unless the audience expects it; define it on first use.
- Verbs over nouns. "Decide" not "Make a decision".
- Cut adverbs that don't add meaning ("really", "very", "actually").

## SEO writing

- Primary keyword in: H1, first 100 words, one H2, meta description.
- Use related terms naturally; don't stuff the keyword.
- Link out to 1–2 authoritative external sources when it helps the reader.
- Link to 2+ internal pages using descriptive anchor text (not "click here").

## Formatting

- Bold for emphasis, sparingly. Italics for titles and foreign terms.
- Bulleted lists for 3+ parallel items; numbered lists for steps.
- Blockquotes for pull quotes or callouts, not for long passages.
- Code blocks with a language tag: ```python, ```bash, etc.
- Tables for comparisons, not for prose.

## Things to avoid

- Generic intros that could open any post.
- Walls of text with no subheadings.
- Unsubstantiated claims ("studies show...") without a source.
- Conclusion that just rephrases the intro. End with a takeaway and a CTA.
